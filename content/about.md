I'm Pourya Mokhtari, a computer engineer at [Sharif University of Technology](https://en.sharif.edu) where I work as a research assistant at the [Information, Network and Learning Labs](https://inl-lab.net).

My research sits at the intersection of distributed systems, applied machine learning, and cryptography. Currently I'm focused on building ML models for anomaly detection in the context of network Intrusion Detection Systems — a problem that is harder than it sounds once you account for the absence of labeled data and the relentless pace of concept drift.

Before that I spent time thinking about consensus protocols, Byzantine fault tolerance, and what it actually means to make a cluster of machines agree on anything under adversarial conditions.
